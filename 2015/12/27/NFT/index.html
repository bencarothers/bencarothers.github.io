Growing plants with your computer

Posted by Ben Carothers on December 27, 2015

Victor

I’ve decided to take on a project pretty far outside of my comfort zone. It’s sort of an extension of my Twipy drip irrigation but much more extensible and intensive. I’m working on creating a Nutrient Film Technique (NFT) hydroponic garden monitored and sustained by a collection of web services hosted on a Raspberry Pi. I’m thinking that I’m going to make each of these restful so that they can be displayed in any number of ways and consumed by many different styles of application.

The project is likely to take a good deal of time, so I should be posting a few more blog posts about the process. I want to keep a log of the build process so that I can see how things change as I learn more.

So far most of my time has been poured into the physical engineering of the garden. I’m still not completely finished, but I’m definitely happy with how it’s coming through. My next step is to get the irrigation rigged up and start messing with some nutrient solutions.

Then once the garden runs with out assistance I’ll probably work on implementing a monitor of water flow – the weakest feature of NFT.
